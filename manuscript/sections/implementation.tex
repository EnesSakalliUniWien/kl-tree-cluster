\section{Implemented Method Specification}

Table~\ref{tab:defaults} records the implemented method specification used by
the repository. These entries determine the exact tree construction,
projection rules, multiplicity corrections, and traversal logic described in
this manuscript.

\begin{table}[ht]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
Component & Implemented choice \\
\midrule
Tree distance & Hamming \\
Linkage & Average linkage \\
Edge significance level & $\alpha_{\mathrm{edge}} = 0.01$ \\
Sibling significance level & $\alpha_{\mathrm{sib}} = 0.01$ \\
Edge test & Spectral projected Wald with eigenvalue whitening \\
Edge dimension rule & Marchenko--Pastur signal count \\
Sibling test & Cousin-adjusted projected Wald \\
Sibling dimension rule & Johnson--Lindenstrauss rule with $\varepsilon = 0.3$ and global floor \\
Minimum spectral dimension & 2 \\
Include internal spectral rows & On \\
Edge multiple-testing correction & Hierarchical BH correction on the tree \\
Sibling multiple-testing correction & Flat BH on focal pairs \\
Pass-through traversal & On \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Implemented method specification in the repository.}
\label{tab:defaults}
\end{table}

Two aspects of the implemented method deserve explicit emphasis. First, the
two tests do not use the same projection rule: the child-parent edge test uses
parent-local spectral whitening, whereas the sibling test uses a random
orthonormal projection with a Johnson--Lindenstrauss dimension cap and then
corrects post-selection inflation through cousin adjustment. Second, the final
clustering stage is intentionally lightweight once the test tables exist:
pass-through traversal is on, so the reported partition is driven directly by
these two inferential tests and the top-down traversal.

The representation layer should also be read as part of the method. Binary data
enter directly, categorical data enter through one-hot indicator expansion, and
continuous data are discretized before the same testing pipeline is applied.
